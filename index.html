Simple Present Tense – Grammar Lesson

The Simple Present Tense is one of the most common verb tenses in English.

We use it to talk about things that happen regularly, facts, and truths. Here are some more details:

  • He / She / It verbs usually add -s or -es at the end.
  • For verbs ending with -ch, -sh, -s, -x, or -z, add -es (e.g., “watches”).
  • For verbs ending with a consonant + y, change y to i and add -es (e.g., “carries”).
  • We use “do” and “does” in negative sentences and questions.

Examples:

  • She does her homework every day.
  • Do you like chocolate?
  • They don’t play football on Sundays.
